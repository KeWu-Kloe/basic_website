Book Conservator

A Book Conservator is primarily responsible for restoring and conserving old or damaged books and manuscripts to ensure that their historical and cultural value is preserved. The work often requires fine manual skills, an understanding of chemistry and materials science, and knowledge of history. A degree in a related field, such as library science, archives, or conservative restoration, is often required for this career, and further specialized training and practical experience may be needed.

Food Stylist

The majority of food stylists are very passionate about what they do and passionate about food in general. In general, food stylists should love to cook and be great at it. They should also have an eye for little details. Individuals interested in a food styling career should be aware that there is no actual degree for food styling specifically.

Knitting is done by using two or more needles to knit thread or wool into cloth. Colorfilling, or coloring, is a popular art activity designed to fill in pre-designed black and white line patterns with color. Suitable for all ages, this activity not only provides creative freedom and individualized expression, but can also help reduce stress and anxiety.
